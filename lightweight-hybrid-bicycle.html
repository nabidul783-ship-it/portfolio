Lightweight Hybrid Bicycle Design

A low-cost urban bicycle concept validated through CAD modeling, SolidWorks simulation, beam checks, material selection, and cost analysis.

Project Summary

A low-cost lightweight hybrid bicycle was designed for urban riding conditions where potholes, deteriorated roads, debris, braking, and repeated loading can create excessive stress and vibration. The goal was to balance structural strength, low displacement, manufacturability, and affordability without adding unnecessary complex components.

The design blended road-bike and mountain-bike characteristics to create a practical urban hybrid. Major components included the frame, fork, handlebar, seat, wheel, and pedal assembly. SolidWorks was used for CAD modeling, engineering drawings, assembly visualization, and simulation studies.

The design was tested using conservative loading assumptions: a 350 lbf rider load, 60/40 front/rear weight distribution, and a 320 lbf braking-force case based on 90% front load transfer and a 0.9 friction coefficient. Each component was checked against material yield strength and a maximum displacement requirement of 1 in.

Technical Process

  • Modeled the bicycle frame and major components in SolidWorks with dimensioned part drawings.
  • Selected 6061-T6 aluminum alloy for most structural components because of its balance of strength, weight, and cost.
  • Ran SolidWorks stress and displacement simulations under worst-case loading assumptions.
  • Supported simulation results with hand calculations and beam-style validation where appropriate.
  • Compared manufacturing cost against the retail range of reliable starter bikes to evaluate affordability.

Results and Outcomes

  • Validated that all simulated components stayed below material yield strength and below the 1 in displacement limit.
  • Reported frame stress of 622 psi and displacement of 0.00323 in under the modeled loading.
  • Reported fork stress of 2,056 psi and displacement of 0.000370 in, plus a simplified beam-case fork stress of 1,973.6 psi and displacement of 0.001222 in.
  • Reported seat stress of 4,763 psi with 0.0086 in displacement, wheel stress of 2,357 psi with 0.004 in displacement, and pedal assembly stress of 52,730 psi with 0.123 in displacement.
  • Estimated a complete design cost of $380, significantly below the $1,000–$3,500 range listed for comparable starter bikes.
