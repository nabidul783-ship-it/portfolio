Project Summary

GripWell was a handheld assistive device designed to help users with tremors keep a tool steady during use. The concept focused on detecting unwanted hand motion, recording motion behavior, and producing a counteracting mechanical response to stabilize the tool.

The prototype used an Arduino controller, gyroscope/IMU sensing, and servo motors. Sensor readings were used to identify tremor motion, while servo actuation provided active correction to reduce tool movement. The final design was 3D printed to create a compact prototype form factor under a tight competition schedule.

The project was completed by a four-person team in only three days, requiring rapid mechanical design, electronics integration, embedded programming, sensor calibration, and iterative physical testing.

Technical Process

  • Defined the use case around tremor compensation for handheld tool stability.
  • Integrated Arduino control with gyroscope/IMU motion sensing and servo motor actuation.
  • Developed a prototype control response that detected tremor-like motion and moved the tool support in the opposing direction.
  • 3D printed the final prototype body to package the electronics and support the tool interface.
  • Tested and adjusted the prototype during the three-day build window to improve steadiness and presentation quality.

Results and Outcomes

  • Built a functional assistive-device prototype within a three-day competition timeline.
  • Led a team of three other students through concept development, prototyping, testing, and final presentation.
  • Demonstrated mechatronics integration across sensing, control, actuation, and 3D-printed mechanical packaging.
  • Tied for 3rd place in the competition.
