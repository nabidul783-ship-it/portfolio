Project Summary

The Thermal Control System was developed for a NASA L’SPACE mission concept where avionics, batteries, sensors, mechanisms, and structural elements needed to remain within safe operating temperatures during extreme Martian environmental conditions. The design combined active heat generation, passive heat distribution, and radiative environmental control into one integrated subsystem.

The system architecture centered on patch heaters, thermostats, solid-state controllers, temperature sensors, thermal straps, a white-painted radiator, and Multi-Layer Insulation. The environmental requirement used Martian ground temperatures from 128 K to 324 K and air temperatures from 149 K to 272 K, with a 10% buffer to account for mission variation and uncertainty.

The design work included requirements development, thermal trade studies, risk identification, redundancy planning, recovery logic, procurement planning, and verification planning. The final concept selected patch heaters for controllable active heating, thermal straps for zero-power heat distribution, and a white top-face radiator with MLI on the remaining faces for controlled heat rejection and retention.

Technical Process

  • Split the subsystem into heat generation, heat distribution, and environmental-interface subassemblies.
  • Compared patch heaters, cartridge heaters, and deployable solar-heating surfaces using weighted criteria for cost, power, mass, size, thermal controllability, and reliability/TRL.
  • Selected thermal straps as the main distribution path because they require no power, contain no fluids or moving parts, and provide flexible heat-transfer routing.
  • Developed redundancy logic with backup patch heaters, alternate thermal strap paths, and sensor fault handling based on disagreements between nearby sensor readings.
  • Prepared procurement and verification plans using off-the-shelf heaters, contractor-built thermal straps, CAD-based mass and volume checks, power measurements, and thermal-vacuum chamber testing.

Results and Outcomes

  • Maintained the thermal concept around a 20°C / 293 K operating target for sensitive internal hardware.
  • Established a complete TCS concept with a documented estimate of $27,846 and a 9-month lead time.
  • Defined a 75 W TCS power limit and inspection/test methods for temperature sensing, passive heating, redundancy, and power draw.
  • Assigned an overall system TRL of 6, with patch heaters, thermostats, and controllers at TRL 7, thermal straps at TRL 6, the white radiator at TRL 8, and MLI at TRL 7.
